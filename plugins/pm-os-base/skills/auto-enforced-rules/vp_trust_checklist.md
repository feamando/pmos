# VP Trust Checklist

**Load this when R11 identifies the audience as stakeholders/VPs/leadership.**

## Two-Layer Trust Architecture

Stakeholder documents must build trust through two distinct layers:

### Layer 1: Main Body (Trust Signals)
The main document body is optimized for **building confidence in the recommendation**. It should:

- **Lead with the insight, not the methodology** -- VPs want to know "what should we do" before "how did you figure that out"
- **Use concrete numbers, not ranges** -- state your base case confidently, footnote the range
- **Show you've considered the counter** -- one sentence acknowledging the strongest objection and why you still recommend this
- **Connect to their goals** -- tie back to OKRs, company strategy, or stated priorities
- **Be scannable in 60 seconds** -- bold the key numbers, use headers that tell the story

### Layer 2: Appendix (Methodology & Rigor)
The appendix exists for **those who want to verify**. It should:

- **Full math chain** -- every intermediate value, every assumption numbered
- **Data sources with dates** -- no undated claims
- **Sensitivity analysis** -- what breaks the case
- **Assumptions table** -- grounded vs speculative, with confidence levels
- **Comparison to prior analysis** -- what changed and why

### The Rule
- **Never lead with methodology** in the main body -- that's appendix material
- **Never omit methodology** from the document -- that's intellectual dishonesty
- The main body earns trust by being clear and confident
- The appendix earns trust by being rigorous and transparent
- A VP who reads only the main body should be able to make the right decision
- A VP who reads both should trust you more, not less

### Anti-Patterns
- Starting with "We analyzed 14 data sources..." (methodology in main body)
- Presenting only optimistic scenario without acknowledging risks
- Using hedge words ("potentially", "might", "could") on grounded claims
- Burying the recommendation on page 4
- Missing attribution on key numbers
