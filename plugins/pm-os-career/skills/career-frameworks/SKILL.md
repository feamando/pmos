# Career Frameworks

## When to Apply

- User discusses career development for a team member
- User asks about promotion readiness or career progression
- User creates or updates career plans
- User asks about competency frameworks or growth areas
- Conversation involves performance review cycles

## What to Do

1. **Use strengths-based coaching** — identify and amplify what each person does well before addressing gaps
2. **Frame growth as trajectory** — where they are, where they're headed, what bridges the gap
3. **Balance task + development** — career plans must include both body of work (WHAT) and behavioral evidence (HOW)
4. **Reference competency dimensions** — map observations to the configured competency framework (default: Speed & Agility, Data-Drivenness, Egolessness, Customer Centricity, Ownership)
5. **Use evidence, not impressions** — cite specific projects, decisions, and feedback entries

## Career Plan Structure

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Background Information | Current role, tenure, ratings, org context |
| The Future Role | Scope change and complexity factors |
| Business Need | Why the promotion matters to the organization |
| WHAT (Body of Work) | 3-5 paragraphs: Scope & Influence, Impact, Ambiguity |
| HOW (Behavioral Evidence) | 3-5 paragraphs: competency framework mapping |
| Key Risks | 2-3 paragraphs: risks and mitigation strategies |
| Peer Feedback | 2-3 peers at target level |

## Cycle Timing

- **S-cycles** (Summer, May-to-May): Primary promotion cycle
- **W-cycles** (Winter): Check-in and course correction
- Auto-detect: Jan-Apr = previous S-cycle, May-Dec = current S-cycle

## Anti-Patterns

- Generic feedback without specific examples
- Focusing only on gaps without acknowledging strengths
- Conflating "busy" with "impactful" — volume of work is not the same as scope and complexity
- Writing career plans in isolation without the team member's input
