# Review Writing Skill

Guidance for writing structured performance reviews calibrated to tone, grounded in evidence, and aligned with organizational frameworks.

## When to Apply

- Writing performance reviews (peer, manager, report, self)
- Assessing evidence against career framework expectations
- Calibrating language to a specific tone/direction

## Tone Calibration (1-5)

| Tone | Label | Evidence Emphasis | Language Register |
|------|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Critical: Immediate Action | Lead with gaps and risks. Achievements brief. | "fell short", "immediate action needed", "significant gaps" |
| 2 | Below Expectations | Lead with improvement areas. Acknowledge positives. | "needs improvement", "inconsistent", "areas of concern" |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Balanced strengths and growth areas equally. | "solid contribution", "consistent delivery", "room to grow" |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Lead with achievements. Growth areas as opportunities. | "strong performance", "notable impact", "impressive results" |
| 5 | Outstanding | Emphasize outsized impact. Gaps as stretch goals. | "exceptional", "role model", "transformative impact" |

## Evidence Standards

Every claim in a review must be grounded in specific evidence:

1. **Cite specifics.** Name the project, ticket, date, or meeting. "Led the OTP checkout launch (GOC-3441, shipped March 2026)" not "led an important project."
2. **Tie to frameworks.** Connect WHAT evidence to career level expectations, HOW evidence to DNA values.
3. **Use the timeframe.** Only cite evidence within the review period. Evidence outside the window is context, not assessment.
4. **Proportional coverage.** Weight evidence sections proportionally to the tone. At tone 4-5, strengths get 70% of space. At tone 1-2, improvement areas get 70%.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Anti-Pattern | Description | Fix |
|--------------|-------------|-----|
| Recency Bias | Over-weighting last 2-4 weeks | Distribute examples across the full timeframe |
| Halo Effect | One strong project colors entire review | Assess each dimension independently |
| Generic Praise | "Great team player" without evidence | Always attach a specific example |
| Sandwich Feedback | Hiding critical feedback between positives | Match structure to tone parameter |
| Level Mismatch | Praising L3 behavior in an L5 review | Reference career framework expectations for the person's level |
| Evidence Gaps | Claiming impact without data | If no evidence exists, note the gap rather than fabricating |

## Writing Guidelines

- Write in second person for report reviews ("You demonstrated..."), first person for self reviews ("I delivered...")
- For peer and manager reviews, use third person ("They showed...")
- Keep paragraphs to 3-5 sentences. One idea per paragraph.
- Lead each section with the strongest evidence point
- Use active voice: "Delivered the migration" not "The migration was delivered"
- Quantify where possible: revenue impact, user metrics, team size, timeline compression
