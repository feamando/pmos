# Organization Values Framework

> **Override:** Set `review.values_framework_path` in config.yaml to use your organization's values framework.
> **Below:** Example values framework. Replace with your own.

## Values

### 1. Speed & Agility

**Definition:** We make quick but informed decisions. We iterate, reiterate and move faster than everybody else. We get things done here.

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Bias for action and realizing impact fast
- Strong experimentation culture; continuously adding value rather than big bangs
- Happy to take calculated risks
- Hands-on problem solving on the spot
- Measures progress in days, not weeks or quarters
- Challenges thinking and setup repeatedly to adapt
- Escalates early when recognizing true misalignment

**Day-to-Day Signals:**
- Moves quickly on priorities without unnecessary bureaucracy
- Reflects on lessons learned before moving ahead
- In the absence of significant test results, looks at leading indicators and runs worst case scenarios
- Keeps end user in mind regardless of what they are working on
- Understands compound effect of small improvements on long-term trajectory

---

### 2. Relentless Prioritization

**Definition:** We stay laser focused on what really moves the needle. We always start with the most important task, set ambitious deadlines, and meet them anyway.

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Uses prioritization as pre-validation
- Recognizes fewer priorities often mean better execution on what matters
- Connects work to overall business goals and KPIs
- Re-prioritizes periodically ("Love planning, not the plan")
- Recognizes tradeoffs; optimizes for global, not local optima
- Acts as owner; wisely prioritizes company resources

**Day-to-Day Signals:**
- Prioritizes to move quickly on most impactful things and avoids busy work
- Gives visibility to others on prioritization decisions
- Flexibly re-prioritizes when data or experiment results change
- Prioritizes long-term success over unsustainable quick-wins
- Everything with direct customer impact usually gets high priority

---

### 3. Egoless Environment

**Definition:** We operate in an environment where we embrace change and challenge the status quo. Best idea wins.

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Success defined by impact, not title/seniority or team size
- Doesn't play politics; doesn't take opposing views personally
- Actively collaborates to achieve best outcomes
- Shares information, tools and best practices proactively
- "Disagree and commit" when alignment is reached
- Values diversity of thought; praises unique contributions
- Leads by example; treats everyone with respect and kindness
- Never says "that's not my job"

**Day-to-Day Signals:**
- Gets hands dirty; gets the job done even if not glamorous
- Embraces change enthusiastically; focuses on new opportunities
- Accepts that what was right in the past may not be right for the future
- Lets data tell when wrong; doesn't get married to a particular idea
- Carefully listens regardless of seniority

---

### 4. Learning Never Stops

**Definition:** We always seek to better ourselves and those around us, on both small and large scale.

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Understands learning happens at all levels: Organization > Department > Team > Individual
- Preserves organizational learning through documentation, retros, knowledge sharing
- Studies industry trends, competitors and best practices
- Champions learning by being a good role model
- Strives to be deep domain expert in their field
- Accepts ambiguity; learns on the job
- Takes ownership of own learning and development

**Day-to-Day Signals:**
- Implements learnings quickly; iterates as new learnings come in
- Uses every failure as an opportunity to learn without defensiveness
- Relies on data to understand the world
- Contextualizes learning from customer point of view
- Builds scalable solutions from what is learned

---

### 5. Data-Drivenness

**Definition:** We are data-driven because data is our vehicle to learn and to make decisions based on fact rather than gut-feeling.

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- "We get what we measure" and "we cannot manage what we do not know"
- Employs analytical rigor in decomposing trends and aggregating results
- Builds hypotheses and verifies/falsifies them with data
- Spends time interpreting data, not only describing it
- Tracks KPIs and metrics in close to real-time
- Communicates clearly what success looks like (OKRs, KPI trees, Goals)

**Day-to-Day Signals:**
- Generates insights early and often to guide actions
- Avoids "analysis paralysis" by focusing on most important metrics
- Puts forward arguments rooted in data and analysis, not gut-feeling or seniority
- Tracks input metrics for continuous learning and outcome metrics for impact
- Focuses on leading indicators which determine long-term performance

---

### 6. Customer Centricity

**Definition:** Every single team has an internal or external customer. We do everything with our customer in mind.

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Clear on who team's customer is and how to best create value for them
- Listens to customers attentively to understand motivations and pain points
- Thinks first about what value customers derive before taking action
- Understands job is only done when customers receive value
- Takes care of people to be able to serve customers
- Aims for positive impact on wider communities and the planet

**Day-to-Day Signals:**
- Solves customers' pain points as soon as possible
- Puts customers' needs first; prioritizes tangible benefits
- Goes the extra mile to serve customers
- Obsesses over how to best serve customers through experimentation, feedback, observations
- Collects data to better understand customers
- Recognizes high customer satisfaction as strongest driver of long-term success

---

### 7. Long-term Orientation

**Definition:** We build for the long-term. We move with speed and agility today to create a scalable and sustainable organization for tomorrow.

**Behavioral Indicators:**
- Understands speed and quality strongly correlate in the long-run
- Utilizes good process and automation for sustainable, repeatable workflows
- Prefers sustainable growth over shortcuts
- Frequently asks if what is built today will serve well in the long-run
- Embraces change without losing sight of long-term vision

**Day-to-Day Signals:**
- Breaks down long-term projects into milestones that can be delivered quickly
- Clearly articulates long-term goals and strategy to manage conflicting priorities
- Accepts occasional steps back to move two steps forward
- Understands organizational learning as a path to long-term success
- Continuously measures tracking toward long-term goals
- Understands that what's best for the customer is what's best for the company long-term
