# Product Management Career Framework

> **Default:** HelloTech Product Management levels
> **Override:** Set `review.career_framework_path` in pm-os config to use a custom framework.

## Level 3: Product Manager (PM)

**Scope:** Owns a feature or a small product area.

**Expectations:**
- **Execution:** Delivers features on time and with quality. Follows established processes.
- **Strategy:** Contributes to the strategy of their area. Understands how their work connects to team goals.
- **Stakeholders:** Works effectively with engineers, designers, and analysts in their squad.
- **Data:** Uses data to inform decisions. Tracks feature-level metrics.
- **Communication:** Writes clear specs. Manages backlog. Keeps stakeholders informed.

**Signals of Strong Performance:**
- Good at writing specs and managing backlog
- Works well with engineering team
- Delivers predictably
- Takes ownership of feature area

---

## Level 4: Senior Product Manager (SPM)

**Scope:** Owns a complex product or a problem space.

**Expectations:**
- **Execution:** Autonomous. Handles ambiguity well. Drives projects to completion without close supervision.
- **Strategy:** Defines the roadmap for their area based on data and customer insights. Connects work to business outcomes.
- **Stakeholders:** Manages cross-functional relationships. Influences without authority.
- **Data:** Sets up measurement frameworks. Uses experimentation rigorously.
- **Communication:** Presents to leadership. Mentors junior PMs. Documents decisions.

**Signals of Strong Performance:**
- Strong prioritization skills; ability to say "no"
- Mentorship of junior PMs
- Data-driven decision making
- Comfortable with ambiguity and complexity

---

## Level 5: Staff / Principal Product Manager

**Scope:** Cross-team initiatives, platform-level products, or new ventures.

**Expectations:**
- **Execution:** Multi-quarter time horizon. Coordinates across tribes and functions.
- **Strategy:** Defines the vision for a domain. Connects product strategy to company strategy.
- **Stakeholders:** Influences senior leadership. Builds alignment across multiple teams.
- **Data:** Defines success metrics for a domain. Drives organizational learning through data.
- **Communication:** Shapes product narratives. Represents product externally.

**Signals of Strong Performance:**
- Systems thinking across multiple product areas
- Influencing without authority at scale
- Strong business acumen and strategic vision
- Ability to break down complex problems into actionable roadmaps

---

## Level 6: Group Product Manager / Director

**Scope:** Manages a portfolio of products and a team of PMs.

**Expectations:**
- **Execution:** Responsible for P&L or key business metrics of the group. Balances short-term and long-term.
- **Strategy:** Sets strategic direction for the organization. Anticipates market shifts.
- **Stakeholders:** Partners with engineering, design, and business leadership. Represents product in exec forums.
- **Data:** Drives data culture across teams. Uses data for strategic decisions.
- **People:** Hires, develops, and retains talent. Organizational design. Succession planning.
- **Communication:** Executive communication. Vision articulation. Cross-functional alignment.

**Signals of Strong Performance:**
- Hiring and developing strong PM talent
- Organizational design and team health
- Executive communication and influence
- Portfolio-level impact and accountability
